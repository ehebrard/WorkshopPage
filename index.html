Overview

This workshop will be held on May 29 2012, in conjunction with CP-AI-OR, and aims to support exchanges between the operation research (OR), constraint programming (CP) and Boolean satisfiability (SAT) communities on the topic of search heuristics and strategies. This is the first international workshop dedicated to search strategies and heuristics for tree-search procedures. Moreover, a special attention will be given to methods considering non standard or complex objective functions such as, but not limited to, solution robustness and fault tolerance, or cost balancing and fairness objectives, or solution sampling and enumeration.

Scope

We welcome articles on emergent and promising generic heuristics and descriptions of dedicated search strategies for a particular application as well as studies and models for less conventional objectives. The scope of this workshop includes, but is not restricted to:
  • Generic branching heuristics and alternative tree search (beam search, limited discrepancy search, etc.);
  • Dedicated search strategies;
  • Alternative search procedures for integer programming (local branching, resolution search, relaxation-induced neighborhood search...)
  • Adaptation of heuristics across different frameworks;
  • Automated search strategies synthesis;
  • Symmetry breaking during search;
  • Solution robustness and fault tolerance;
  • Cost balancing and fairness;
  • Solutions sampling, diversity, regularity, stability;
